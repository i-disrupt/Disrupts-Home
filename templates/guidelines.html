Disrupt Community Guidelines Effective: August 8th, 2022 Last Updates: August 8th, 2022

Welcome to Disrupts! (formerly known to you as DisruptAI)

We have created Disrupts / DisruptAI to assist people in their everyday tasks, and to help increase their productivity with our services. Our Guidelines helps keep everything nice and clean so that other people can enjoy our service too.
These guidelines explain what is and what is not allowed on Disrupts / DisruptAI. Everyone must abide by these rules as they apply to all parts of our service, including the content you share with our community, account behavior, etc. We have the right to consider relevant off-platform behavior related to our service when assessing for violations of our rules.
Moderators, Administrators, and people of related status on our team may review, edit, and / or report the content you've shared on your account. Including, but not limited to Names, Plugins, Usernames, etc. If we've come to the conclusion tha you have violated any of these guidelines, we may suspend your account.

RESPECT DISRUPTS

- Do not use DISRUPTS to harm / damage someone else's experience
- Do not create content that include Racism, Sexually explicit content, the glorification or promotion of self-harm or worse, or attempts at damaging one's mental state.
- Do not create content containing misleading information or misinformation.
- Do not abuse Disrupts / DisruptAI products in any way
- Do not inappropriately engage in the community. (e.g Sexualising members of the community, Spread hateful content, purposefully attempt to ruin one's day, Slurs or discriminative content, etc)
- Do not attempt to mislead Moderators.
- Do not provide misinformation or joke content in official forms. (e.g Application forms, etc.)
- Refunding purchases will result in a permenant ban from our service.


These guidelines will continue to evolve over time. This means we may take action against a user, server, or content that violates the spirit of these guidelines when we encounter a new threat or harm that is not explicitly covered in the current version.
